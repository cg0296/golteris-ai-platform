# Freight Quote Workflow

## Overview

This workflow describes how a freight quote request moves from a customer to a broker, then out to carriers, and back to the customer as a final priced quote.

## 1. Customer Sends Request for Quote

The client or customer sends the broker a request for quote in natural language, usually by email.

The request may include:

- Origin and destination for the freight movement
- Truck type requirements
- Insurance requirements
- Driver responsibilities
- Lane specifications
- Number of trucks needed
- Pricing expectations such as:
  - Price per lane
  - Price per truck per lane
  - Average price per truck

A freight movement from point A to point B is referred to as a **lane**.

## 2. Broker Reviews the Request

The broker reviews the incoming request for quote to confirm that all required information is present.

If information is missing, the broker follows up with the customer to gather the remaining details needed to prepare a complete quote request.

The broker's goal at this stage is to make sure the lane requirements are clear enough to request pricing from carriers that can move the freight.

## 3. Broker Standardizes the Request

Once the request is complete, the broker converts the natural language request into a structured format, typically a spreadsheet.

The spreadsheet usually includes:

- Lane details
- Number of trucks required
- Equipment or truck type
- Lane specifications
- Insurance requirements
- Driver responsibilities
- Any other service or delivery requirements

This structured format allows the broker to send the same request consistently to multiple carriers.

## 4. Broker Sends Quote Request to Carriers

The broker sends the structured lane request to several carriers to obtain pricing.

At this stage, the broker is effectively asking carriers what price they would accept to move the freight from origin to destination under the stated requirements.

## 5. Carriers Review and Respond

Carriers review the lanes and determine whether they can service them.

They respond to the broker with their pricing, which may be returned in different formats, including:

- Natural language email responses
- Completed spreadsheets

Carrier responses typically include the price they want in order to perform the delivery for the requested lane.

## 6. Broker Compares and Negotiates Bids

After receiving responses from multiple carriers, the broker:

- Reviews the bids
- Compares pricing and service fit
- Negotiates where needed
- Selects the best carrier bid or combination of bids

The selection is based on both commercial and operational fit, not just the lowest price.

## 7. Broker Applies Markup

After selecting the preferred carrier pricing, the broker applies a markup percentage.

This markup becomes the broker's final sell price to the customer.

## 8. Broker Sends Final Quote to Customer

The broker sends the final quoted pricing back to the customer.

The final customer quote may be presented as:

- Price per lane
- Price per truck per lane
- Average price per truck

## Simple End-to-End Flow

1. Customer sends natural language RFQ to broker.
2. Broker reviews the request for completeness.
3. Broker requests missing information if needed.
4. Broker converts the request into a structured spreadsheet.
5. Broker sends the lane request to multiple carriers.
6. Carriers review the request and return bids.
7. Broker reviews, negotiates, and selects the best bid.
8. Broker marks up the selected carrier price.
9. Broker sends final pricing to the customer.
