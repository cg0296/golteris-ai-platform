What actually happens inside the broker's manual process. Every step is human-driven, email-dependent, and serial.

You are looking inside: Broker — Email + Manual Process (Black Box)

This is the center hub from the architecture diagram. Customers and carriers connect to this from the outside.

1. Read & Interpret Emails

Broker opens each email and mentally extracts: origin, destination, truck type, insurance, lane specs, truck count, pricing expectations. No parsing — pure human reading.

Tools: Outlook / Gmail

2. Check Completeness

Broker checks if all required fields are present based on experience and memory. No checklist, no validation rules. Easy to miss something.

Tools: Broker's memory

3. Compose Follow-up

If info is missing, broker manually writes an email asking the customer for specifics. Then waits for a reply — could be hours or days.

Tools: Outlook / Gmail

4. Build Spreadsheet

Broker manually retypes email content into a spreadsheet: lane details, truck count, equipment, insurance, driver responsibilities. Copy/paste and typo-prone.

Tools: Excel / Google Sheets

5. Distribute to Carriers

Broker attaches the spreadsheet and composes individual emails to each carrier. May forget a carrier or send the wrong version.

Tools: Outlook / Gmail + Excel

6. Compare Bids

As bid emails arrive, broker reads each one, extracts the pricing, and manually enters it into a comparison spreadsheet. Different carriers use different formats.

Tools: Outlook / Gmail + Excel

7. Negotiate & Select

Broker evaluates bids on cost, service fit, and reliability. May go back and forth with carriers via email. Selection criteria lives in broker's head.

Tools: Outlook / Gmail + judgment

8. Calculate Markup

Broker applies a markup percentage to the selected carrier price in the spreadsheet. Manual formula — no rules engine, no guardrails.

Tools: Excel / Google Sheets

9. Compose Quote Email

Broker writes the final quote email to the customer with the marked-up pricing. Formatted as price per lane, per truck per lane, or average — depending on what the customer asked for. Sent manually.

Tools: Outlook / Gmail
